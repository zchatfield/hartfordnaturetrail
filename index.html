South Hartford Natural Spaces

Welcome!

The city of Hartford, Connecticut has a wealth of public green spaces. We invite you to explore the parks, community gardens, and natural hidden gems in the Southern part of Hartford.

Click on each green space to learn more about it or scroll down to see more information on each green space. Use the search bar in the top left to look for your favorite spot. Click here to explore routes from any location to your destination.

This page is a collaborative project brought to you by Lauren Little Edutainment and Free Center. It was created by Zoe Chatfield and Emily Heltzel of Clark University.

The Brookfield Wetlands are a nature preserve located in the Behind the Rocks neighborhood of Hartford. They border the South Brook section of the Park River.
There's so much to learn about the parks and wetlands hidden in the city!

Cedar Hill Cemetery is more than just a place of burial. It has beautiful grounds for walking, biking, and exploring. You can learn more from their website here. Enjoy the view of the Llyn Mawr pond and their open green space, called "ornamental foreground" on their visitors map! Beyond this area, you will find a short nature walk through a lightly wooded part of the cemetary grounds.

Cemetery Brook Natural Trail is located behind the Environmental Sciences Magnet School. Access it by bike or walking, and enjoy learning about another hidden water feature in Hartford!

Charter Oak Landing is just South of Downtown Hartford, and is along the Connecticut River. It includes a playground, boat launch, and connects to Great River Park with the Charter Oak Bridge pedestrian walkway (ADD TO MAP?).

Nature highlights:
Nesting eagles.

Colt Park is a 114 acre park, established in 1905. It features walking paths, ponds, fountains, a playground, splash pad, and public swimming pool. Learn more about the current advocacy to restore the park here.


Free Center is a community space located in the former Goodwin Library building.
Here, you can find a flower garden, daffodils planted as part of the city of Hartford's community hope garden initiative, raised beds growing fruits, vegetables, and herbs, and their edible landscape.

Goodwin Park extends from South Hartford into Wethersfield. On its 237 acres, you can find recreation areas for tennis, basketball, and softball, as well as a golf course. It's nature features include ponds and walking trails. Learn more about its history and features here.

Thomas J. Hyland Memorial Park has a playground, two baseball fields, a basketball court, a tee-ball field, and a soccer field. It's across from Rocky Ridge Park, and between these two areas, there's space to play, walk, and explore!

Pope Park was donated to the City of Hartford in 1895 by Colonel Albert Pope. The 79 acre park has extensive green spaces, walking trails, a pond, two athletic fields, and a rec center.

Garden highlight: There is a community garden available to residents. Yearly registration for garden beds occur.
Nature highlight: Learn about their tree planting master plan here.

Porter Memorial Park is a small public green space in the South Green neighborhood.

Rice Heights Playground is a small park in the Behind the Rocks neighborhood. It's located next to Breakthrough Magnet School. There is a playground, baskebtall court, and baseball field.

Rocky Ridge Park has a football and softball field. It's right by Hyland Memorial Park and Trinity College. It also has some lightly wooded areas to explore! Learn more from Real Hartford's blog post about this hidden gem.

Summer of Solutions runs a small community garden and free food pantry. They offer an internship program for youth that focuses on urban agriculture, mutual aid, and food justice. Support them on Patreon and learn more about them here.

The Trinity College Community Garden is a project aimed at providing Hartford families with the ability to grow their own food. Plots are located on Broad Street, next to the Trinfo Cafe.

Nature highlight: There are also beehives here! Bees are an important pollinator and help keep our gardens and broader ecosystem healthy.

Zion Hill Cemetery is located in the Frog Hollow neighborhood. This historic green space offers an opportunity to wander its paths. The Friends of Zion Hill have worked to beautify this space. Enjoy the flowers they've planted and check for future public gardening opportunities!
