South Hartford Nature Trail

Welcome!

The city of Hartford, Connecticut has a wealth of public green spaces. We invite you to explore the parks, community gardens, and natural hidden gems in the Southern part of Hartford.

Click on each green space to learn more about it or scroll down to see more information on each green space. Use the search bar in the top left to look for your favorite spot. Click here to explore routes from any location to your destination.

This page is a collaborative project brought to you by Lauren Little Edutainment and Free Center. It was created by Zoe Chatfield and Emily Heltzel of Clark University.

The Brookfield Wetlands are a nature preserve located in the Behind the Rocks neighborhood of Hartford. They border the South Brook section of the Park River.
There's so much to learn about the parks and wetlands hidden in the city!

Cedar Hill Cemetary is more than just a place of burial. It has beautiful grounds for walking, biking, and exploring. You can learn more from their website here. Enjoy the view of the Llyn Mawr pond and their open green space, called "ornamental foreground" on their visitors map! Beyond this area, you will find a short nature walk through a lightly wooded part of the cemetary grounds.

Cemetary Brook Natural Trail is located behind the Environmental Sciences Magnet School. Access it by bike or walking, and enjoy learning about another hidden water feature in Hartford!

Charter Oak Landing is just South of Downtown Hartford, and is along the Connecticut River. It includes a playground, boat launch, and connects to Great River Park with the Charter Oak Bridge pedestrian walkway (ADD TO MAP?).

Colt Park is a 114 acre park, established in 1905. It features walking paths, ponds, fountains, a playground, splash pad, and public swimming pool. Learn more about the current advocacy to restore the park here.

Free Center is a community space located in the former Goodwin Library building.
Here, you can find a flower garden, daffodils planted as part of the city of Hartford's community hope garden initiative, raised beds growing fruits, vegetables, and herbs, and their edible landscape.
